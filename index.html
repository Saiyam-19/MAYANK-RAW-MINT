Compression Fittings

Used wherever an easy and economical method of connecting tubing is required like instrumentation, hydraulic and pneumatic systems, and are designed for use with aluminium, copper and plastic tubing.

Read More

Flare Fittings

Extensively used for fuel, oil, air liquid petroleum(LP) and natural gas lines connections. Can be used with copper, brass, aluminium and welded steel tubing that can be flared.

Read More

Pipe Fittings

Used in applications including water, grease, fuels, LP/natural gas, refrigeration, instrumentation and hydraulic systems. Can be connected to brass, copper and iron pipe in low/medium pressure connections.

Read More

Hose Barb Fittings

Hose barbs are continuous ridges or bumps on a fitting that are used to grip the inside diameter of a tube and seal the connection. They dominate the connecting flexible tubing market.

Read More

Garden Hose Fittings

They are flexible synthetic rubbers/plastics reinforced with internal webs of fibres. They're smooth to wrap around obstacles while also being tough enough to step on without damage or leaking.

Read More

Push On Barb Fittings

Fittings have sharper barbs than standard barbed hose fittings to grip rubber push-on hose with no clamps or ferrules required. The more you pull on the fittings, the tighter the hose will grip.

Read More

Steel Fittings

Steel fittings are components used in piping systems to connect and control the flow of liquids or gases. These fittings are typically made from steel, a robust and durable material that is well-suited for various industrial applications.

Read More

Pex Fittings

PEX (cross-linked polyethylene) fittings are used in plumbing systems for connecting PEX tubing, which is a flexible and durable piping material. PEX is known for its flexibility, corrosion resistance, and ease of installation, making it a popular choice for both residential and commercial plumbing applications.

Read More

Stainless Steel Fittings

Stainless steel fittings are components used in piping systems, valves, and other applications where corrosion resistance and durability are crucial. Stainless steel is an alloy of iron with a minimum of 10.5% chromium content, which forms a protective oxide layer on the surface, making it highly resistant to corrosion.
